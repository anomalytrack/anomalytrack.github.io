Introduction

Networks today provide critical services, and a significant amount is spent on their operation and maintenance. However, many current systems still depend on human interaction to function properly.

With society’s increasing reliance on these networks, failure events have become unacceptable.

In scenarios ranging from autonomous vehicles to remote surgeries, a single network failure can not only result in fines but also damage the credibility of an entire company.

In a real commercial network, the number of KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) can reach into the hundreds of thousands. Efficiently monitoring all these indicators is a complex challenge, often approached ineffectively. So, how can this problem be solved?

What is the Anomaly track?

AnomalyTrack is a adaptative monitoring system that uses IA to define anomalies in complex anomaly networks.

Created during a master's research at Brazil's top university (ITA) and with a paper set to be published at one of the leading conferences, the concept was born out of a real market need—one personally experienced by the current directors of ZTN.

What makes AnomalyTrack different from other monitoring systems?

Other monitoring systems rely on manually defined thresholds to function and typically monitor only a small portion of the network. AnomalyTrack, on the other hand, is designed to monitor 100% of the network KPIs without the need for manual threshold configuration, which significantly improves anomaly detection performance.

And how does AnomalyTrack work?

The tool's engine is capable of filtering the KPIs that are relevant within the network’s context. Then, its anomaly detection module can identify events even without prior alarm data.
